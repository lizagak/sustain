Hi there! I'm {{ site.author }}.

I am an fifth year PhD candidate at the School of Information at UC Berkeley, where I am advised by Dr. Niloufar Salehi. I am currently on the job market for postdoc and industry positions!

Most recently, I published The Distressing Ads That Persist: Uncovering The Harms of Targeted Weight-Loss Ads Among Users with Histories of Disordered Eating at CSCW 2022.

I am interested broadly in human-computer interaction, online harms, social and relational aspects of privacy, and interpersonal surveillance. I use interviews, ethnography, speculative design, and participatory action research methods to engage young people as co-researchers in reflecting on their everyday experiences with technology. My research has been generously funded by the National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellowship, the Center for Technology, Society, and Policy, and the Algorithmic Fairness and Opacity Group. At Berkeley, I've had the pleasure of teaching INFO 188: Behind the Data - Humans and Values with Prof. Deirdre Mulligan as well as INFO 203: Social Issues of Information with Dr. Morgan Ames. In the past, I have worked at Meta, AT&T, and US Bank.

I graduated from Washington University in St. Louis, studying Computer Science, Mathematics, and American Culture Studies. In undergrad, I worked with Dr. Karrie Karahalios and Dr. Ranjitha Kumar at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.


Outside of work, you can find me playing tennis, building my latest stained glass piece, volunteering as a concert usher, and organizing Yiddish culture events across the Bay Area. Contact me at @{{ site.twitter }} or by email. My CV is available upon request.
